That doesn't look like the Milky Way, it looks like a dot!

This is a zoomed out view of the universe, showing how the Milky Way is part of a larger supercluster of galaxies. Each dot represents a galaxy while each line shows the direction the galaxy is moving.

The galaxies in this supercluster, Laniakea, are ditinct from the rest becuase they are all moving toward the "Great Attractor" where all the lines meet. Eventually, Laniakea will dissipate and we will become part of a different supercluster.
